## Position

Pass on the $100K fast check now, or gate it behind reconciled commercial evidence. Independently verified shipping velocity [sig-002, sig-003, sig-005] is real but insufficient to underwrite a paid wedge when the deck’s scale and revenue claims are subject-sourced only [sig-001] and directly contradicted on install count by public registry data [sig-004].

## Case

1. **The traction narrative fails an independent sanity check.** Deck claims 3,100 *active* installations [sig-001], yet the public package registry shows only 2,900 *lifetime* downloads total [sig-004]. Even generous reconciliation (mirrors, on-prem, alternate channels) cannot erase the structural problem: lifetime downloads below claimed active installs implies either definitional inflation (“install” ≠ durable use) or overstated scale. **[high]** — this is not a rounding error; it is a serious credibility flag on the commercial story the check would be pricing in.

2. **All revenue traction is founder-claimed with zero corroboration.** $18k MRR and 40% MoM growth for three months exist only in the deck [sig-001], trust tier “claimed,” authority “subject.” For a pre-seed devtools company asking us to believe early PMF, uncorroborated MRR is not a supporting detail—it is the entire commercial thesis. We would be writing a check against a number the founder authored with no Stripe export, no customer references tied to spend, and no third-party validation. **[high]**

3. **Observable adoption proxies point to nascent, not scaled, capture.** Registry velocity is 340 downloads/week and 2,900 lifetime [sig-004]; GitHub shows 412 stars and 61 forks [sig-002]; launch engagement was strong but ephemeral (214 points, three self-identified production users in comments) [sig-005]. These independently verified signals are consistent with a credible early OSS project—not with 240 organizations running paid or production-critical workloads at the scale implied by deck economics ($18k MRR ÷ 240 orgs ≈ $75/org/month, which itself strains plausibility without named accounts). **[medium]**

4. **Market remains neutral because the budget line is already owned.** Structural agent-CI cost pressure is real, but remote caching is a crowded layer: Bazel/Gradle remote cache, GitHub Actions cache, Turborepo/Nx already sit where Ferrite would need to win procurement. Ferrite’s wedge (content-addressed dedup across agent fleets) is plausible in deck positioning [sig-001] but unproven as a standalone budget item versus incumbents absorbing the workload. Verified shipping [sig-002] does not prove wedge entry into a paid category—it proves a founder can build. **[medium]**

5. **Execution conviction is priced; commercial verification is the missing leg.** Founder axis reads positively on cold-start execution (73 band, anchored on [sig-002, sig-003, sig-005, sig-006]), and idea-vs-market “survives-as-is” on mechanism coherence [sig-002, sig-004]. But the forum crux is explicit: the axes *disagree* on whether shipping velocity alone satisfies the fund’s appetite for pre-track-record founders when commercial scale is contested. A fast check exists precisely to avoid diligence theater—here, the contested items *are* the diligence: installs, MRR, design-partner production usage. Granting $100K on execution alone converts “pre-track-record tolerance” into “pre-traction tolerance,” which is not what the written thesis describes when revenue and install claims are load-bearing. **[medium]**

6. **Reference and benchmark claims remain entirely subject-sourced.** Two unnamed design partners (Series-B devtools, AI lab), 10x cost reduction, and prior fintech remote-cache outcomes all flow from deck or founder narrative [sig-001], with no independent anchor. Hackathon win [sig-003] validates technical judgment under competition, not enterprise willingness to pay or retention at scale. We would be stacking unverified commercial claims atop verified build evidence—a ladder violation if those claims support valuation or urgency. **[high]**

## Concessions

- **Real product, real founder output:** 1,400 commits, weekly releases, external contributors, and CI with test coverage are independently verified [sig-002]; this is not vapor.
- **Independent technical validation:** Hack-Nation infrastructure track win against ~400 teams [sig-003] and substantive launch-thread engagement with identified production users [sig-005] support technical credibility and early organic interest.
- **Mechanism coherence:** Content-addressed remote caching is a proven primitive; the agent-fleet angle is a coherent extension, not category fantasy [sig-002, sig-004]—registry tension caps scale claims but does not invalidate the idea.
- **Thesis fit:** EU pre-seed devtools/AI-infra with a technical solo founder is in scope; the bear case is not “wrong category,” it is “wrong time to write an uncorroborated commercial check.”

## Decisive test

**Stripe (or payment-processor) export for trailing 3 months, cross-walked to a product analytics export defining “active installation” (install vs. active-weekly-user, including self-hosted/on-prem not in [sig-004]).** If reconciled figures show ≥$12k MRR with ≥2 named paying accounts and an active-install count consistent with registry + off-registry distribution (or a documented, auditable gap explanation), I move from pass to conditional yes on the fast check. If MRR is materially lower, installs reconcile closer to registry-scale adoption [sig-004], or “active installation” collapses into one-time pulls, commercial traction remains a hard gate regardless of shipping velocity [sig-002].
